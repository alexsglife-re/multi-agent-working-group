## Why

Leader active-state and handoff documents can grow from a compact control record into thousands of lines, increasing takeover cost and the chance that current decisions are obscured by history. A single fixed line cap is unsafe and poorly matched to both small and large projects, so the workflow needs progressive, evidence-preserving profiles that control active-state growth without truncating required facts or adding large-project ceremony to ordinary small work.

## What Changes

- Add provisional Compact and Standard active-state profiles selected from current active complexity, with a lightweight, reviewable selection basis.
- Make complete applicable accepted state-carrying requirements the non-truncatable retention floor; size never authorizes omission, inherited authority, or assumed approval.
- Preserve the existing layered record in one physical active-state instance while referencing durable evidence, raw output, and historical notes by stable pointer.
- Add provisional structural classification thresholds, warning-only line/byte baselines, profile transition rules, and event-driven freshness checkpoints. Structural thresholds may change the profile but never block work by themselves; semantic and control failures remain fail-closed from the start.
- Add profile-aware Compact/Standard handoff rendering and successor projection requirements that preserve the accepted Opportunity versus Recommended/Required rollover behavior.
- Add structural, fixture-based local validation without claiming semantic truth, authorization validity, lifecycle execution, or factual correctness.
- Record `hierarchical-required` when Standard is insufficient, while explicitly deferring Hierarchical root/card/shard design and enforceable calibrated thresholds to separate follow-on changes.
- Declare `v1.0.0 — Progressive Leader State Profiles` as the authorized development target while keeping `v0.4.18` as the current public version until a separately gated public release-status closeout.

## Capabilities

### New Capabilities

None.

### Modified Capabilities

- `leader-state-compaction`: Define progressive Compact and Standard profiles, selection evidence, retention precedence, transition behavior, and the interim `hierarchical-required` state.
- `copyable-role-templates`: Require genuinely reduced profile-aware active-state and handoff instances while preserving every applicable accepted field.
- `leader-rollover-protocol`: Define profile-aware successor projection without changing rollover triggers or allowing authority inheritance.
- `local-validation-tool`: Add marker, structure, measurement, and fixture checks for profiles while preserving the validator's non-semantic boundary.
- `public-release-status`: Track the Owner-authorized v1.0.0 development target separately from the current public v0.4.18 release.

## Impact

- Affects `SKILL.md`, Leader-state and handoff references, copyable templates or a deterministic renderer, representative examples, local validation fixtures, and validation documentation.
- Does not implement Hierarchical sharding, automatic/background compaction, destructive cleanup, semantic policy validation, or enforceable numeric size gates.
- Does not change deployment, release, secret, authorization, permission, schema, or destructive-operation boundaries.
- Version-development status changes do not authorize a tag, GitHub Release, public release-status conversion, deployment, or external publication; those remain separate gates.
- The reported prior document growth beyond 4,000 lines is Owner-reported continuity evidence; if the original inventory is unavailable, validation will use clearly labeled synthetic stress fixtures rather than claim a historical reconstruction.
